About Me

I’m Kemper! An aerospace engineering student—which is a fancy way of saying I like making things go really fast and, ideally, not explode. I joke. When I’m not buried under equations designed to test my sanity, I’m diving into projects in coding, creative writing, and video content creation. I combine technical expertise in areas like systems, programming, and physics with a creative mindset. With skills in web development, video editing, machine learning, and problem-solving, I work to merge logic with imagination, always eager to learn and create something innovative and impactful.

You see, I like learning everything. One moment, I’m studying quantum mechanics and the next I’m tweaking code and before I know it I’m deep in a video project wondering why I thought DaVinci Resolve was my friend.

Coding is my way of playing god—except instead of creating life, I create infinitely spawning error messages. There’s something poetic about writing a hundred lines of logic, pressing run, and immediately being greeted by a cryptic error message that suggests I should reconsider my life choices, but that’s the fun of it! Every bug fixed is a small victory, and every project finished is another reason to celebrate.

Writing, however, is where raw thought flourishes. Through it, I am able to dissect concepts, unraveling complexity into digestible meaning. Fiction, technical analysis, philosophical musings—each piece is a reflection of my fascination with the universe; a testament to the interplay between logic and imagination. Ever wonder why time slows near a black hole? Or why you age slightly faster on a plane than on the ground? No? That’s fine—I’ll still happily rant about relativity to anyone willing to listen. To me, physics isn’t just numbers and formulas; it’s the poetry of the universe, a blueprint holding the secrets to existence—an artistry of understanding that fuels both my writing and scientific curiosity.

Video editing too I can bring thoughts to life in a visual and dynamic way. It's not just about cutting and pasting clips—it's about understanding rhythm, emotion, and timing to evoke a response from the viewer.

So, what do you get when you mix aerospace engineering, coding, video editing, physics, and a passion for storytelling?

You get me—an over-caffeinated, sleep-deprived, mildly existential nerd who just wants to understand the universe and create cool things along the way.

If time is relative, I’d like to kindly ask it to slow down when I have a deadline. Thanks in advance.
